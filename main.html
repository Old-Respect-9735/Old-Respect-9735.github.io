Mission Statement


The Problem

According to a new study by the World Health Organization (WHO), the COVID-19 pandemic has led to a 25% increase in the prevalence of anxiety and depression worldwide. The study found that women, young people, and people with pre-existing mental health conditions were particularly affected. The study also highlighted the need for governments and organizations to prioritize mental health services and support during and after the pandemic. WHO recommends measures such as promoting mental health literacy, increasing funding for mental health services, and addressing the social determinants of mental health to mitigate the impact of the pandemic on mental health.

How does Oasis address this issue?

Our idea is to implement an ingenious website called Oasis which assesses the mental health of the individual. Using OpenCv, we can determine whether the user is unhappy or happy. Using a Machine Learning model, we are also predicting the future depression rates to raise caution in individuals and prevent people from being depressed. In addition to these solutions, we also have a mental health survey which utilizes a variety of factors in depression to figure out what type of help the person needs. Additionally, the increase in the dependency of technology has impacted millions of children. With more and more children being stuck to their screen, there is a significant decrease in social interaction and an increase in depression. As a result, our website will reach out to the younger generation and help them achieve a happier mood or a better outlook on life.
